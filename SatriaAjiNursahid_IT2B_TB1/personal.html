Background

I am currently a first-year Information Technology student at Politeknik Negeri Jakarta, where I spend most of my days decoding the complexities of software and network logic. My journey into the world of tech started with a simple curiosity about how the digital tools we use daily actually function under the hood. Choosing a vocational path at PNJ was a deliberate move; I wanted an environment that values hands-on practice as much as theory. Right now, I’m in the "grind phase," navigating through my first semester of intense coding labs and foundational projects, all while trying to master the art of turning coffee into functional syntax. When I’m not in the lab, you’ll likely find me diving into the world of Cybersecurity or contributing to Open Source projects. I’ve always been drawn to the "detective work" side of IT, finding vulnerabilities and understanding how to build more resilient systems. I’m that teammate who actually enjoys debugging, seeing every error message as a puzzle rather than a setback. Whether it’s a late-night hackathon or a casual Discord study session, I thrive in collaborative environments where we can break things down and build them back better than they were before. Outside of the terminal, I try to keep my life balanced by exploring street photography and competitive gaming. I find that framing a shot through a camera lens helps me clear my head, giving me a fresh perspective that I can bring back to my UI/UX designs or logic structures. For me, being an IT student isn't just about the lines of code I write; it’s about the mindset of constant problem-solving and adaptation. I’m just at the beginning of my path here at PNJ, but I’m fully committed to sharpening my skills to eventually make a real impact in the tech industry.
